About Daniel

Daniel is a collegiately-trained vocalist, pianist, and a certified instructor of the Institute for Vocal Advancement (IVA). With an extensive background in music and a never-ending need for knowledge, he is a versatile vocalist, a proficient pianist, and an informed instructor who is constantly pursuing continuing education to effectively help singers achieve their vocal and musical goals. His disposition is calm, thoughtful, and positive, creating a casual, yet focused environment.

Daniel currently studies voice with Rebecca Lam, a professional vocalist and certified IVA instructor, and Spencer Welch, a Master Teacher and Director of Education for IVA. After experiencing profoundly positive results with his voice, Daniel decided to become certified with IVA (Institute for Vocal Advancement), an international non-profit organization that offers both exceptional vocal education and a certification program for singing instructors worldwide.

Daniel holds a bachelor’s degree in music from Western Washington University, and has experience in multiple styles, including pop, jazz, R&B, soul, folk, and classical. During his undergraduate studies, he performed solo arias with the Whatcom Symphony Orchestra and WWU Chorale, sang the role of Don Pedro in WWU Opera Studio's production of W.A. Mozart's Don Giovanni, and gave a solo voice recital with pianist Madeline Slettedahl. Daniel was one of three semi-finalist nominees for the Virginia Glover WWU Music Award, indicating musical excellence in a 75-student voice department. He also performed the national anthem and alma mater at his university’s commencement ceremony. Outside an active performing career, Daniel has explored and excelled in many supporting musical disciplines, receiving top marks in piano, theory, conducting, diction/phonetics, and jazz improvisation.

Lessons

Each voice lesson consists of a targeted vocal workout using goal-specific IVA exercises designed for your unique voice. Clients can also spend time on repertoire, foreign language diction, sight-reading, or performance/recording preparation.

The IVA technique can help anyone sing in a free and natural manner, smoothly, from the bottom to the top of the range with no breaks or sudden changes in quality. It helps improve a singer's range, breath control, resonance, endurance, stamina, vocal quality and strength. Since technique is distinctively separate from style, this method will help vocalists of any genre of music. Solid voice technique is extremely important for complete artistic freedom, which is the ultimate objective.
